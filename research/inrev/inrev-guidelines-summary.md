# INREV Guidelines and Commercial Real Estate Intelligence: Comprehensive Research Summary

**Research Date:** January 2026
**Focus Area:** INREV (European Association for Investors in Non-Listed Real Estate Vehicles) standards, guidelines, tools, and best practices for institutional real estate investment intelligence.

---

## Executive Summary

INREV is the European Association for Investors in Non-Listed Real Estate Vehicles with over 500 corporate members including institutional investors, fund managers, consultants, and advisors. INREV develops comprehensive guidelines and tools to standardize reporting, governance, and transparency across the non-listed real estate investment industry. The organization provides 9 modules of best practice guidelines covering reporting, governance, valuation, liquidity, performance measurement, sustainability, fees, NAV calculation, and data delivery standards.

---

## 1. INREV Guidelines Overview

### Structure and Organization

The INREV Guidelines consist of **9 modules** providing modular, tiered adoption of best practices:

1. **Reporting** - Comprehensive disclosure requirements for annual/interim reporting
2. **Governance** - Corporate governance, board structure, oversight, and controls
3. **Property Valuation** - Valuation methodologies, oversight, and assessment procedures
4. **Liquidity** - Redemption policies, stress testing, and liquidity management
5. **Performance Measurement** - TWR, IRR, and standardized performance metrics
6. **INREV NAV** - Net Asset Value calculation and disclosure standards
7. **Fee and Expense Metrics** - Fee disclosure, TGER (Total Global Expense Ratio), REER (Real Estate Expense Ratio)
8. **Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG)** - Sustainability reporting framework and KPIs
9. **Risk Management** - Operational and investment risk frameworks

### Application Timeline

- Effective for reporting periods **beginning January 1, 2024** (earlier adoption encouraged)
- Property Valuation guidelines updated January 2023
- Governance guidelines updated January 2022
- ESG SDDS released November 2023

### Adoption Framework

INREV provides a modular approach allowing vehicles to adopt best practices at appropriate levels. Vehicles can be assessed against these modules through the **INREV Compliance Assessments** tool, receiving scores that can be shared with investors for peer comparison.

## 2. INREV Standard Data Delivery Sheet (SDDS)

### Overview and Purpose

The **Standard Data Delivery Sheet (SDDS)** is a standardized reporting template that facilitates efficient information exchange between investment managers and investors. Since its first release in 2012, the SDDS has become the industry standard for non-listed real estate data reporting.

### SDDS 4.0 - Latest Version (2023)

Key features of SDDS 4.0:
- **Vehicle-level data**: Key terms, financial data, investor information
- **Asset-level data**: NEW in version 4.0 - granular property-specific information
- **Global definitions**: Consistent terminology and data standards across regions
- **Standardized format**: Enables easy upload into investor systems for analysis and comparison
- **Reduced duplication**: Streamlines data exchange between managers and investors
- **Enhanced comparability**: Allows peer-to-peer performance and structure comparison

### SDDS Components

**Financial Data Fields:**
- NAV calculations and adjustments
- Performance metrics (TWR, IRR, SI-IRR)
- Fee structures (management fees, performance fees, expenses)
- Liquidity activity (subscriptions, redemptions, transfers)
- Capital calls and distributions

**Vehicle Information:**
- Vehicle structure and terms
- Investor base information
- Strategy and objectives
- Key service providers

**Asset-Level Information:**
- Property characteristics
- Valuation data
- Occupancy and performance
- ESG metrics and sustainability data

### ESG SDDS Extension (November 2023)

- **Purpose**: Standardize ESG Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) disclosure
- **Coverage**: Environmental, social, and governance metrics aligned with SFDR, GRI, UN PRI, TCFD, and GRESB
- **Integration**: Works alongside financial SDDS for comprehensive reporting
- **Comparability**: Enables investors to benchmark ESG performance across portfolios

### Benefits and Impact

- **Reduced reporting burden**: Single standardized template vs. multiple custom reports
- **Faster analysis**: Pre-formatted data ready for institutional investor systems
- **Data quality**: Consistent definitions and calculation methodologies
- **Efficiency**: Estimated 30-40% time reduction in data processing

## 3. NAV Calculation and Reporting Standards

### INREV NAV Guidelines

**Objective:**
Provide managers with guidance on calculating and disclosing INREV NAV in financial reports, leading to transparency and comparability across European non-listed real estate vehicles.

### Framework Basis

- Based on **IFRS** (International Financial Reporting Standards)
- Provides industry-specific adjustments framework
- Applicable to **both open-end and closed-end vehicles**
- Enables calculation of meaningful "adjusted NAV"

### Key NAV Components

**NAV Calculation Process:**

1. **Base NAV**: Net asset value calculated per financial statements
2. **Adjustments**: Industry-specific adjustments for real estate vehicles
3. **Reconciliation**: Full transparency of adjustments to IFRS net assets
4. **Disclosure**: Clear presentation of calculation and assumptions

**Standard Adjustments Include:**
- Property valuation methodologies and market value assessment
- Deferred tax provisions (specific to real estate structures)
- Side letter terms and their financial impact
- Liquidity adjustments and redemption provisions
- Fee impacts on net asset value

### Reporting Requirements

**Frequency:**
- Annual reporting (minimum)
- Interim reporting (quarterly or semi-annual, per vehicle structure)

**Disclosure Requirements:**
- Full reconciliation of adjusted NAV to IFRS net assets
- Detailed explanation of each adjustment
- Comparative periods (YoY comparison)
- Asset-level breakdown of values

### SDDS NAV Fields Integration

Each NAV reporting requirement is cross-referenced to relevant SDDS data fields, enabling automated data flow from reporting systems to investor databases.

## 4. Performance Measurement Standards

### INREV Performance Measurement Module

**Objective:**
Support investment managers in computing and reporting standardized performance measures at vehicle and asset levels, increasing consistency in investor reporting and alignment with global standards.

### Consistency with Global Standards

- Aligned with **Global Investment Performance Standards (GIPS)** (CFA Institute)
- Consistent with **NCREIF PREA Reporting Standards**
- Modified-Dietz methodology for TWR calculations
- IRR methodology consistent with NPV calculations

### Performance Metrics: TWR vs. IRR

#### Time-Weighted Return (TWR)

**Use Case:**
- Open-end vehicles where manager doesn't control capital flows
- Non-discretionary single-client portfolios
- Manager performance evaluation

**Reporting Requirements:**
- Mandatory for open-end vehicles
- Report periods: 1-year, 3-year, 5-year, 10-year annualized (where history exists)
- Since inception performance (annualized)
- Exact day-count methodology accepted for returns >= 1 year

**Calculation:**
- Uses Modified-Dietz method
- Adjusts for the timing and size of cash flows
- Isolates manager performance from investor cash flow decisions

**Example Application:**
Core/Core+ commingled funds with continuous subscriptions and redemptions use TWR to demonstrate manager skill independently of investor timing.

#### Internal Rate of Return (SI-IRR)

**Use Case:**
- Closed-end vehicles where manager controls capital deployment
- Investment performance measurement
- Alignment with investor economics

**Key Characteristics:**
- Measures rate of return that results in zero net present value
- Reflects actual investor returns including timing impact
- Standard for closed-end fund reporting

**Reporting:**
- Preferred metric for closed-end vehicles
- TWR optional but not required for closed-end funds
- Gross and net IRR reporting

**Example Application:**
Closed-end opportunity funds report SI-IRR reflecting actual capital deployment schedule and distribution timing.

### Vehicle-Level Performance Reporting

**Annual Report Disclosure:**
- Total vehicle return on time-weighted basis
- Performance across required time periods
- Gross and net returns
- Benchmark comparisons (where applicable)
- Explanation of calculation methodologies

### Asset-Level Performance

- Component returns by property or asset
- Quarterly performance updates (minimum)
- Attribution analysis where applicable

### Performance Attribution

Guidelines support attribution reporting for:
- Manager contributions to performance
- Market factors vs. active management factors
- Risk-adjusted return analysis

**Governance Metrics:**
- Board composition and diversity
- Ethics and compliance programs
- Risk management framework implementation

- **SFDR** (Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation) - EU requirements
- **GRI** (Global Reporting Initiative) - Voluntary disclosure standards
- **UN PRI** (Principles for Responsible Investment) - Investor commitments
- **TCFD** (Task Force on Climate-related Financial Disclosures) - Climate reporting
- **GRESB** (Global Real Estate Sustainability Benchmark) - Industry benchmarking

**Features:**
- Standardized data template for ESG KPIs
- Harmonized metrics across investor bases
- Comparable ESG performance data

**Data Fields:**

## 8. Fee and Expense Metrics

### INREV Fee and Expense Module

**Objective:**
Clarify calculation and disclosure of standardized fee metrics, enabling comparisons between vehicles and with other investment structures.

### Standardized Fee Metrics

#### Total Global Expense Ratio (TGER)

**Definition:**
Comprehensive measure of all costs borne by the vehicle and its investors, expressed as percentage of average NAV.

**Components:**
- Management fees (all types)
- Performance fees
- Operating expenses
- Service provider costs (custody, audit, valuation, administration)
- Travel and communication expenses
- Regulatory and compliance costs
- Recurring and non-recurring costs

**Calculation:**
- Total annual expenses / Average NAV over period
- Annualized for partial periods
- Disclosed in basis points (bps)
- Net-of-fee basis

**Reporting:**
- Annual TGER
- Multi-year comparison
- Explanation of significant year-over-year changes
- Benchmark comparisons where applicable

#### Real Estate Expense Ratio (REER)

**Definition:**
Subset of TGER excluding real estate operating expenses, focusing on vehicle management and administration costs.

**Components:**
- Management fees
- Performance fees
- Administrative costs (custody, audit, administration)
- Regulatory and compliance costs

**Exclusions:**
- Direct property operating costs (utilities, maintenance, management fees paid to third-party operators)
- Property taxes
- Insurance
- Capex and maintenance

**Use Case:**
Enables comparison of management efficiency while excluding property-specific costs that vary by strategy and asset class.

### Fee Disclosure Framework

**Required Disclosures:**
- Clear identification of all fees and their purposes
- Management fee structure (flat, tiered, performance-based)
- Performance fee calculation methodology
- Fee benchmarking against peer vehicles
- Historical fee trends (3-5 years minimum)
- Fee waivers or caps (where applicable)
- Fee changes and effective dates

**Fee Categories:**
- Base management fees
- Performance/incentive fees
- Administrator and trustee fees
- Audit and accounting fees
- Legal and professional fees
- Valuation and appraisal fees
- Custody and brokerage fees
- Operational expenses
- One-time or non-recurring costs

### Fee Governance Best Practices

- Annual fee review and benchmarking
- Independent governance approval
- Investor communication on fee reasonableness
- Fee transparency in marketing materials
- Regular disclosure of fee changes
- Clear explanation of calculations

## 9. Data Delivery and Reporting Standards

### INREV Data Delivery Module

**Objective:**
Standardize data delivery processes and formats to enhance efficiency, comparability, and quality of information exchange.

### Data Delivery Frequency

**Standard Requirements:**
- **Annual**: Comprehensive full SDDS reporting within specified timeframe post-year-end
- **Interim**: Quarterly or semi-annual depending on vehicle structure
- **Special Events**: Redemption activity, capital calls, distributions, changes in strategy

**Reporting Timeline:**
- Typically 90 days post-year-end for annual SDDS
- 30-45 days for interim reports
- Faster delivery for critical information (liquidity events, redemptions)

### Data Quality Standards

**Accuracy Requirements:**
- Data should be accurate, consistent, and verifiable
- Reconciliation with audited financial statements
- Cross-validation of calculations
- Source documentation available for audit

**Completeness:**
- All SDDS fields populated per guidelines
- Explanations provided for missing or N/A data
- Notes and caveats clearly disclosed
- Material changes flagged and explained

**Consistency:**
- Uniform calculation methodologies across periods
- Consistent definitions per INREV Global Definitions Database
- Historical comparability
- Explanation of any changes in methodology

### Global Definitions Database (GDD)

**Purpose:**
Provides standardized definitions for all data elements in SDDS, ensuring consistent interpretation and calculation across organizations and jurisdictions.

**Coverage:**
- Financial metrics (NAV, returns, fees, expenses)
- Valuation terms and methodologies
- Liquidity definitions
- ESG indicators
- Governance and risk terms
- Property types and categories

**Usage:**
- Mandatory reference for SDDS completion
- Training and onboarding resource
- System implementation reference
- Audit and compliance verification

### Data Delivery Technology Integration

**System Requirements:**
- SDDS formatted in standardized file formats (Excel, XML, data feed)
- API availability for automated data retrieval
- Reconciliation controls and audit trails
- Version control and amendment tracking
- Data security and encryption standards

## 10. Compliance, Assessment, and Governance Frameworks

### INREV Compliance Assessments

**Overview:**
INREV provides self-assessment tools enabling vehicles to evaluate compliance against 7 of the 9 guideline modules, producing scores for investor communication and peer comparison.

### Assessment Modules (7 of 9 Assessed)

1. **Reporting Compliance** - Disclosure requirements checklist
2. **Governance Compliance** - Board structure and processes
3. **Property Valuation Compliance** - Valuation oversight and procedures
4. **Liquidity Compliance** - Redemption policies and documentation
5. **Performance Measurement Compliance** - Calculation and disclosure standards
6. **Fee and Expense Metrics Compliance** - Fee disclosure completeness
7. **ESG/Sustainability Compliance** - ESG strategy and KPI reporting

### Assessment Process

**Completion:**
- Vehicle managers complete self-assessment questionnaire
- Evidence and documentation referenced
- Scoring: Percentage of requirements met (0-100%)
- Results aggregated by module

**Vehicle Dashboard:**
- Track progress across assessments
- Assign permissions to team members
- View results per module or aggregate score
- Compare against peer vehicles
- Monitor compliance trends over time

### Assessment Output and Usage

**Compliance Scores:**
- 0-100% for each module assessed
- Partial compliance clearly identified
- Gap analysis provided
- Remediation recommendations

**Investor Communication:**
- Share aggregate compliance score with investors
- Use in marketing materials and pitch books
- Annual reassessment demonstrates continued compliance
- Third-party benchmark comparisons

**Peer Benchmarking:**
- Compare against vehicles of similar size/strategy
- Identify industry best practices
- Track market-wide compliance trends

### Reporting Module Compliance Checklist

**Detailed Checklist:**
The Reporting module provides a comprehensive checklist summarizing disclosure requirements:
- Annual report contents and format
- Interim report requirements
- Performance metric disclosure
- Fee and expense transparency
- NAV calculation and presentation
- ESG reporting requirements
- Governance disclosure requirements
- Risk and liquidity reporting
- Management discussion and analysis requirements

**Compliance Validation:**
- All checklist items must be fully implemented for full module compliance claim
- Partial compliance documented and disclosed
- Regular updates as guidelines evolve

## 14. Key Takeaways and Strategic Recommendations

### For Commercial Real Estate Intelligence Systems

**1. Data Standardization is Critical**
- SDDS 4.0 provides the foundation for standardized data exchange
- ESG SDDS enables comparable sustainability metrics
- Global Definitions Database ensures consistent interpretation
- Investing in SDDS-compliant data infrastructure is essential

**2. Multi-Module Compliance Approach**
- Adopt modular, phased implementation of guidelines
- Start with Reporting and Governance foundations
- Layer in Performance, Fee, ESG, and Valuation modules
- Use Assessments for progress tracking and investor communication

**3. Automation Drives Efficiency and Quality**
- Manual compliance processes are becoming insufficient
- AI and automation can reduce reporting burden by 40-80% across functions
- Machine learning improves forecasting and risk identification
- Real-time monitoring replaces point-in-time assessments

**4. ESG Integration is Mandatory**
- SFDR, TCFD, and GRESB alignment increasingly expected
- ESG SDDS provides standardized reporting template
- Material ESG risks must be integrated into investment decision-making
- Transparent ESG communication is competitive advantage

**5. Governance and Transparency are Investor Expectations**
- Independent board oversight and clear communication are baseline expectations
- Fee transparency and benchmarking are critical for investor retention
- Liquidity and redemption policies must be clearly documented
- Consistent reporting builds investor trust and reduces due diligence burden

**6. Technology Integration Enables Competitive Advantage**
- Automated SDDS generation from operational systems
- Real-time performance and ESG dashboards for investors
- Predictive analytics for risk identification and modeling
- Enhanced investor engagement through interactive reporting

### Implementation Priorities

**Immediate (0-3 Months):**
1. Assess current compliance against INREV modules
2. Map existing data to SDDS fields
3. Identify system and process gaps
4. Develop remediation roadmap

**Short-term (3-6 Months):**
1. Implement SDDS reporting processes
2. Establish governance and board structures per guidelines
3. Develop performance measurement and reporting
4. Create investor communication protocols

**Medium-term (6-12 Months):**
1. Build ESG data infrastructure and SDDS integration
2. Automate NAV calculation and fee metrics
3. Develop investor dashboard and reporting platforms
4. Complete first full INREV compliance assessment

**Long-term (12+ Months):**
1. Implement AI/ML for forecasting and risk analysis
2. Develop predictive ESG scoring
3. Achieve full compliance across modules
4. Benchmark performance against peer institutions

## 15. Conclusion

INREV guidelines provide the comprehensive framework for institutional real estate investment intelligence, encompassing governance, reporting, performance measurement, sustainability, and risk management. The modular approach enables flexible adoption while the standardized tools (SDDS, DDQ, Global Definitions Database) facilitate efficient information exchange.

The evolution from manual processes to AI-enabled automation represents a significant opportunity for real estate investment managers to differentiate through superior data quality, faster reporting, and enhanced investor communication. Organizations that effectively implement INREV standards while leveraging emerging technologies will establish competitive advantages in an increasingly data-driven and transparent institutional investment landscape.

The standardization movement driven by INREV, ESG regulatory requirements, and investor demand for transparency will continue to accelerate, making INREV compliance a market baseline rather than a competitive advantage. The next wave of competition will focus on how organizations leverage standardized data for superior insights, predictive analytics, and investor engagement.
