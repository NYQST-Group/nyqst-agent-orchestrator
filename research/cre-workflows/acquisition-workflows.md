# Commercial Real Estate Acquisition Workflows: Comprehensive Research

## Executive Summary

Commercial real estate acquisition is a complex, multi-phase process involving hundreds of potential deals screened to find a single viable investment. The process spans 30-90 days (sometimes up to 6 months) and involves multiple stakeholders including acquisition leads, brokers, attorneys, engineers, property managers, and investment committees. This research documents the detailed workflows, data requirements, pain points, and opportunities for AI-assisted automation across the entire acquisition lifecycle.

---

## 1. DEAL SOURCING

### 1.1 Overview

Deal sourcing operates as a funnel where deals enter at the top and are subjected to various screens and criteria, with only the most promising deals emerging for vigorous pursuit. Sponsors may look at hundreds of potential investment opportunities before choosing to move forward with one particular acquisition.

### 1.2 Sourcing Channels

#### On-Market Deals
- **Broker Networks**: Primary source for marketed properties
- **Digital Platforms**: CoStar, LoopNet, Reonomy, Yardi Matrix
- **Auction Platforms**: Online marketplaces for distressed or bulk assets
- **Structured Feeds**: Tools like Dealpath Connect inject property listings from leading global brokerages directly into buyer pipelines

#### Off-Market Deals
- **Direct Outreach**: Data-driven approach to identify property owners with upcoming loan maturities or acquisition needs
- **County Records**: Research property ownership and debt maturity dates
- **Network Referrals**: Owners, bankers, insurance agents, property managers, fellow investors
- **Relationship-Driven**: Local, relationship-dependent process requiring outreach to multiple contacts

#### Inbound vs. Outbound Strategies
- **Inbound**: Content marketing, SEO for commercial terms, digital presence, social media, referrals from past clients
- **Outbound**: Proactive targeting using data services and direct contact campaigns

### 1.3 Information Memorandums (IMs/OMs)

#### Purpose
An offering memorandum is a presentation and marketing document given to investors highlighting various aspects of the investment opportunity.

#### Typical Structure and Content

**Executive Summary**
- Property overview and investment highlights
- Location and accessibility
- Key financial metrics snapshot

**Property Details**
- Physical description and specifications
- Site plan and building layout
- High-quality photographs and renderings
- Demographic trends and area analysis

**Financial Information**
- Historical operating statements (typically 3+ years)
- Current rent roll
- Expense breakdown
- Financial projections and pro forma
- Comparable sales/rentals
- Valuation metrics (Cap Rate, IRR, Equity Multiple)

**Legal and Compliance**
- Confidentiality disclaimers
- Risk factors
- Regulatory considerations
- Investment terms and structure
- Disclaimer of warranties

**Market Analysis**
- Submarket overview
- Supply and demand dynamics
- Comparable properties
- Growth projections and trends

**Lease Summary** (for income-producing properties)
- Tenant roster and credit profiles
- Lease expiration schedule
- Rental rate analysis
- Occupancy history

#### Review Process

**Stage 1: Initial Screening (Financial)**
- Verify financial calculations
- Cross-reference data for consistency
- Check cap rates against market comparables
- Validate revenue and expense figures

**Stage 2: Market Validation**
- Verify market statistics
- Research demographic claims
- Validate comparable properties
- Assess growth projections for realism

**Stage 3: Document Quality**
- Ensure narrative flows smoothly
- Check formatting consistency
- Verify alignment of messaging throughout
- Confirm all legal requirements are met
- Professional proofreading

**Stage 4: Gap Analysis**
- Review previous OMs investor reception
- Identify information gaps
- Note recurring questions or requests
- Flag areas needing more data

### 1.4 Initial Screening Criteria

#### High-Level Filters
- **Asset Class**: Office, retail, industrial, multifamily, mixed-use
- **Geography**: Target markets and submarkets
- **Size**: Square footage or unit count thresholds
- **Price Range**: Total consideration and per-unit/SF metrics
- **Investment Thesis**: Core, core-plus, value-add, opportunistic

#### Financial Screens
- **Yield Requirements**: Minimum cap rate, IRR, cash-on-cash return
- **Leverage Constraints**: Maximum LTV, debt service coverage
- **Hold Period**: Target investment horizon compatibility
- **Return Metrics**: IRR targets, equity multiple thresholds

#### Operational Screens
- **Occupancy**: Minimum stabilized occupancy levels
- **Tenant Quality**: Credit profiles and concentration limits
- **Lease Rollover**: Exposure within investment period
- **Deferred Maintenance**: Capital expenditure requirements

### 1.5 CRM and Deal Pipeline Management

**Centralized Data Management**

**Workflow Automation**
- Automatic lead assignment
- Status update triggers and reminders
- Task creation and delegation
- Deadline tracking and alerts

**Reporting and Analytics**
- Deal velocity metrics
- Conversion rates by stage
- Source effectiveness analysis

#### Popular Platforms
- **Specialized CRE**: Dealpath, AscendixRE, CREPipeline, Yardi Deal Manager, Altrio, 4Degrees, Occupier

## 2. DUE DILIGENCE

### 2.1 Overview

Due diligence is the structured process of verifying everything the seller and marketing brochure claim—from legal ownership and building integrity to tenant performance and zoning compliance. It's critical for identifying hidden factors such as outstanding liens, deferred maintenance, environmental issues, and tenant risks that can make the difference between an excellent deal and an expensive mistake.

**Timeline**: Typically 30-90 days, can extend to 6 months for complex deals
**Start Date**: Clock starts when both parties sign the Purchase and Sale Agreement (PSA)

### 2.2 Core Team Members

| Role | Responsibilities |
|------|------------------|
| **Acquisition Lead/Broker** | Drives the process, interfaces with seller, tracks deadlines, coordinates team |
| **Real Estate Attorney** | Reviews title, PSA clauses, zoning, legal documents, closing requirements |
| **Environmental Engineer** | Conducts Phase I ESA, commissions Phase II if needed, remediation planning |
| **Building Engineer/Inspector** | Evaluates HVAC, roof, structure, ADA compliance, capital repair budget |
| **Lease Auditor/Property Manager** | Verifies rent roll, estoppels, CAM reconciliations, tenant creditworthiness |
| **Financial Analyst** | Models cash flows, validates historical financials, sensitivity analysis |

### 2.3 Legal Due Diligence

#### Purpose
Legal due diligence evaluates the property to confirm its value as represented and uncover any problem areas that might need to be addressed before closing.

#### Title Review (Most Critical)

**Title Examination Process**
- Attorney and title insurance company provide "commitment" to issue title policy
- Ensures seller can deliver clear title
- Reviews chain of ownership
- Identifies any clouds on title

**Title Report Contents**
- Current ownership verification
- Easements (access, utility, drainage)
- Covenants and restrictions
- Liens and encumbrances
- Rights of way
- Recorded agreements

**Title Insurance**
- Owner's policy protects buyer
- Lender's policy protects mortgage holder
- Coverage for defects in title
- Legal defense against claims

#### Survey Review
- Confirms property boundaries
- Identifies encroachments
- Verifies setback compliance
- Shows easements and access rights
- Notes topographic features
- ALTA/NSPS survey standard for commercial transactions

#### Lease Review and Abstraction

**Lease Abstraction Process**
Lease abstracts summarize key terms of leases in a uniform format, making it easy to quickly understand important points without reading full lease documents.

**Key Information Extracted:**
- Property address
- Landlord and tenant information
- Guarantor details
- Premises description and square footage
- Lease commencement and expiration dates
- Base rent and escalation clauses
- Operating expense passthroughs (CAM, taxes, insurance)
- Option rights (renewal, expansion, termination)
- Tenant improvement allowances
- Parking allocations
- Exclusive use clauses
- Assignment and subletting restrictions
- Default provisions and cure periods
- Landlord and tenant maintenance obligations

**Tenant Estoppel Certificates**
- Certified statement by tenant verifying lease terms
- Confirms current status of lease
- States monthly rent payment amount
- Verifies payment commencement
- Confirms any termination rights
- Declares default status (neither party)
- Verifies security deposit held
- Used to confirm accuracy of rent roll
- Critical for acquisition financing

#### Contract Review

**Service Contracts**
- Property management agreements
- Maintenance contracts (HVAC, elevator, landscaping)
- Security services
- Janitorial services
- Parking management
- Waste removal
- Review assignability and termination rights

**Third-Party Agreements**
- Ground leases
- Reciprocal easement agreements (REAs)
- Operating agreements
- Development agreements
- Joint venture documents

#### Litigation Review
- Search for pending litigation
- Review foreclosure proceedings status
- Identify potential claims
- Assess impact on transaction
- Note that recent litigation may not yet appear on title report

#### Zoning and Regulatory Compliance
- Current zoning classification
- Permitted uses
- Building code compliance
- Occupancy permits
- Certificate of occupancy validity
- Conditional use permits
- Variance requirements
- ADA compliance status

### 2.4 Technical Due Diligence

#### Property Condition Assessment (PCA)

**Purpose**
Technical Due Diligence (TDD) is a comprehensive pre-acquisition assessment examining fabric, structure, services, statutory compliance, and potential future liabilities, providing a clear view of risks, costs, and opportunities.

**Building Systems Assessed:**

**Structural Systems**
- Foundation integrity
- Structural framing
- Load-bearing walls and columns
- Floor systems
- Roof structure and condition
- Exterior envelope (walls, windows, waterproofing)
- Parking structure condition

**Mechanical Systems (HVAC)**
- Heating systems age and condition
- Cooling systems and chillers
- Ventilation and air quality
- Controls and building automation
- Equipment service history
- Remaining useful life estimates
- Energy efficiency ratings

**Electrical Systems**
- Service capacity and panels
- Distribution systems
- Emergency power/generators
- Lighting systems
- Fire alarm systems
- Building management systems
- Code compliance

**Plumbing Systems**
- Domestic water supply
- Sanitary drainage
- Storm water management
- Water heaters
- Sprinkler systems
- Backflow prevention

**Life Safety Systems**
- Fire suppression systems
- Fire alarm and detection
- Emergency lighting
- Exit signage and routes
- Smoke control systems
- Emergency communication systems

**Other Building Components**
- Elevators and escalators
- Doors and hardware
- Interior finishes
- Flooring systems
- Accessibility features

#### Engineering Reports

**Structural Engineer Report**
- Foundation assessment
- Structural load capacity
- Seismic considerations
- Observed defects
- Recommended repairs

**M&E (Mechanical & Electrical) Engineer Report**
- System-by-system evaluation
- Design criteria review
- Maintenance and servicing obligations
- Lease arrangement impacts
- Allocation of responsibilities
- Operating cost projections

**Roof Consultant Report**
- Roof system type and age
- Current condition assessment
- Leak history
- Remaining useful life
- Repair vs. replacement recommendations
- Warranty status

#### Capital Expenditure (CapEx) Assessment

**Immediate Needs** (0-1 year)
- Critical repairs required
- Safety issues
- Code violations
- Failed systems

**Short-Term CapEx** (1-3 years)
- Deferred maintenance items
- Systems approaching end of useful life
- Planned improvements

**Long-Term CapEx** (3-10 years)
- Replacement reserve planning
- Major system overhauls
- Building upgrades

**CapEx Reserve Methodology**
- **Item-by-Item Basis**: Identify major components, estimate remaining useful life and replacement cost, set aside proportionate amount annually
- **Example**: $10,000 roof with 20-year life = $500/year reserve
- **Common Reserve Levels**: 1-2% of purchase price annually, or 5-15% of gross rent depending on property age and condition

### 2.5 Environmental Due Diligence

#### Phase I Environmental Site Assessment (ESA)

**Purpose**
Identifies potential environmental contamination liabilities through historical research and site inspection (no soil sampling).

**Components:**
- **Site History**: Historical use research, aerial photographs, fire insurance maps
- **Site Inspection**: Visual observation of property and surroundings
- **Records Review**: Government databases, environmental agencies, contamination reports
- **Interviews**: Current/former owners, occupants, neighbors, government officials
- **Adjacent Properties**: Assessment of neighboring site impacts

**Common Issues Identified:**
- Underground storage tanks (USTs)
- Asbestos-containing materials (ACMs)
- Lead-based paint
- PCB-containing transformers
- Soil contamination
- Groundwater contamination
- Vapor intrusion risks
- Historical industrial use

#### Phase II Environmental Site Assessment

**Triggered When**: Phase I identifies Recognized Environmental Conditions (RECs)

**Activities:**
- Soil sampling and analysis
- Groundwater monitoring wells
- Air quality testing
- Building material sampling
- Laboratory analysis
- Risk assessment
- Remediation cost estimates

**Deliverables:**
- Contamination extent mapping
- Remediation options and costs
- Regulatory compliance pathway
- Impact on property value
- Transaction structure implications (price adjustment, escrow, indemnification)

#### Environmental Compliance Review
- **Hazardous Materials Management**: Storage, handling, disposal practices
- **Stormwater Management**: Permits, best management practices
- **Air Permits**: HVAC, generators, industrial processes
- **Waste Management**: Solid waste, recycling, hazardous waste
- **Regulatory Violations**: Notice of violations, outstanding orders

### 2.6 Financial Due Diligence

#### Historical Operating Statement Analysis

**Income Verification**
- **Rent Roll Reconciliation**: Compare actual collected rent vs. scheduled rent
- **Bank Statement Cross-Check**: Verify reported income with actual deposits (when possible)
- **Lease Audit**: Confirm rent rates, escalations, and terms match leases
- **Other Income**: Parking, storage, laundry, vending, antenna/signage leases
- **Vacancy Analysis**: Historical trends, seasonal patterns
- **Concessions**: Rent abatements, tenant improvements, leasing commissions
- **Bad Debt**: Collection issues and write-offs

**Operating Expense Verification**
- **3+ Years of Statements**: Request detailed P&L statements, tax returns
- **Expense Categories**:
  - Real estate taxes
  - Insurance (property, liability, flood, earthquake)
  - Utilities (electric, gas, water, sewer)
  - Property management fees
  - Repairs and maintenance
  - Payroll (if on-site staff)
  - Landscaping and snow removal
  - Elevator/HVAC maintenance contracts
  - Security
  - Legal and accounting
  - Marketing and leasing costs

**Owner vs. Typical Operator Analysis**
- Identify non-recurring expenses
- Adjust for market-rate management fees
- Normalize one-time capital items
- Benchmark against comparable properties

**CAM (Common Area Maintenance) Reconciliations**
- Review tenant CAM charges and reconciliations
- Verify pass-through expense recovery
- Identify over/under recovery patterns
- Check for disputes or audit rights

#### Capital Expenditure History
- **Historical CapEx**: 3-5 years of major repairs/improvements
- **Deferred Maintenance**: Items postponed by seller
- **Planned Improvements**: Seller's projected future needs
- **Warranty Status**: Existing warranties on major systems

**Future CapEx Projections**
- Engineer-informed replacement schedules
- Reserve requirement modeling
- Impact on cash flow projections

### 2.7 Commercial Due Diligence

#### Market Analysis

**Submarket Assessment**
- Supply and demand fundamentals
- Absorption rates
- Rental rate trends
- Vacancy rates and trends
- Development pipeline
- Competing properties
- Demographic trends
- Employment and economic drivers
- Transportation and infrastructure

**Property Positioning**
- Competitive set identification
- Relative strengths/weaknesses
- Market share analysis
- Pricing positioning
- Value-add opportunities

#### Tenant Credit Analysis

**Framework: The Five Cs**

1. **Character**: Reputation and payment history
2. **Capacity**: Financial strength and debt levels
3. **Capital**: Investment in lease improvements
4. **Conditions**: Market impacts on tenant performance
5. **Collateral**: Guarantees that reduce default risk

**Credit Evaluation Process**
- Request tenant financial statements (if applicable)
- Review Dun & Bradstreet or other credit reports
- Check business references
- Analyze industry trends and tenant business model
- Assess lease terms relative to tenant strength
- Evaluate personal guarantees

**Credit Tenant Leases (CTL)**
- Long-term leases (typically 10+ years)
- Nationally recognized tenant with high credit rating
- Loan based on tenant's ability to pay rent rather than building value
- Investment-grade ratings preferred
- CTL finance dependent on tenant creditworthiness

**Tenant Concentration Risk**
- Percentage of income from largest tenant(s)
- Lease expiration clustering
- Industry concentration
- Geographic diversification of tenant base

#### Lease Terms Analysis

**Key Lease Provisions**
- **Rent Structure**: Base rent, percentage rent, escalations
- **Expense Structures**: Gross, modified gross, triple net (NNN)
- **Renewal Options**: Terms, rental rate determination, notice requirements
- **Expansion Rights**: ROFO, ROFR on adjacent space
- **Termination Options**: Early out clauses, conditions
- **Assignment/Subletting**: Restrictions, consent requirements, profit sharing
- **Tenant Improvement Allowances**: Amount, timing, control
- **Free Rent Periods**: Abatements and their impact
- **Co-Tenancy Clauses**: Required co-tenants, rent reduction remedies
- **Exclusive Use**: Category restrictions on landlord
- **Hours of Operation**: Minimum requirements
- **Kick-Out Clauses**: Performance-based termination rights

**Lease Rollover Analysis**
- Expiration schedule
- Renewal probability assessment
- Mark-to-market potential (current vs. market rents)
- Re-leasing costs (downtime, TI, commissions)
- Rollover risk concentration

## 3. UNDERWRITING

### 3.1 Overview

Underwriting is the in-depth financial analysis combining current property data (occupancy, rental income, property taxes, utilities) with assumptions related to future performance. The process is designed to be critical and eliminate deals that pose unacceptable risk or don't meet investment criteria.

**Typical Phases:**
1. **Incoming Deal Flow**: Initial deal entry and cataloging
2. **Initial Screen**: Quick pass/fail against core criteria
3. **Preliminary Underwrite**: Detailed financial modeling and analysis
4. **Investment Committee Review**: Presentation and debate of merits
5. **Final Due Diligence**: Post-approval deep dive before closing

### 3.2 Financial Modeling Practices

#### Core Valuation Metrics

**Net Operating Income (NOI)**
- Formula: Effective Gross Income - Operating Expenses
- Key metric for property valuation
- Excludes debt service and capital expenditures
- Basis for most valuation approaches

**Capitalization Rate (Cap Rate)**
- Formula: NOI / Purchase Price
- Market-driven metric for direct capitalization
- Varies by asset class, location, quality, risk profile
- Used for property valuation: Value = NOI / Cap Rate

**Internal Rate of Return (IRR)**
- Time-weighted return considering all cash flows
- Accounts for timing of capital deployment and returns
- Target IRRs vary by strategy:
  - Core: 6-9%
  - Core Plus: 9-12%
  - Value-Add: 12-18%
  - Opportunistic: 18%+

**Equity Multiple**
- Total cash returned / Total equity invested
- Simple measure of multiple on invested capital
- Typically 1.5x-2.5x for value-add deals over 5-7 years

**Cash-on-Cash Return**
- Annual pre-tax cash flow / Total equity invested
- Year-over-year yield metric
- Useful for comparing current return to alternatives

**Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR)**
- NOI / Annual Debt Service
- Lender requirement typically 1.20x-1.35x
- Measures ability to service debt from operations

#### Modeling Structure

**Revenue Projections**
- **Base Rent**: By tenant or unit, incorporating contractual escalations
- **Rental Rate Growth**: Market-driven assumptions for renewals and new leases
- **Rent Roll**: Detailed tenant-by-tenant or unit-by-unit schedule
- **Lease Expiration Schedule**: Rollover timing and mark-to-market analysis
- **Vacancy Assumptions**: Physical vacancy plus credit loss
- **Renewal Probability**: Likelihood of lease renewals
- **Other Income**: Parking, storage, utilities, amenities, fees

**Operating Expenses**
- **Fixed Expenses**: Real estate taxes, insurance (typically growing 2-4%/year)
- **Variable Expenses**: Utilities, repairs, management (often % of revenue)
- **Expense Recovery**: CAM reimbursements from tenants (NNN vs. gross leases)
- **Expense Ratio**: Operating expenses as % of Effective Gross Income
- **Benchmarking**: Compare to comparable properties and industry standards

**Capital Expenditures**
- **Acquisition CapEx**: Immediate repairs, deferred maintenance, initial upgrades
- **Ongoing CapEx**: Annual reserves (typically $0.10-$0.50/SF or 1-2% of revenue)
- **Tenant Improvements (TI)**: Allowances for new/renewing tenants
- **Leasing Commissions**: Brokerage fees for new leases and renewals
- **Major Replacements**: Roof, HVAC, parking lot, elevator modernization

**Financing Assumptions**
- **Loan-to-Value (LTV)**: Typically 55-75% for acquisition
- **Interest Rate**: Fixed vs. floating, spread over benchmark rate
- **Amortization**: 25-30 years common, interest-only periods
- **Term**: 5, 7, 10-year terms typical
- **Prepayment Penalties**: Yield maintenance, defeasance, step-downs
- **Debt Fees**: Origination, legal, appraisal, environmental

**Exit Assumptions**
- **Hold Period**: Typically 5-10 years for modeling
- **Terminal Cap Rate**: Usually 25-50 bps higher than going-in cap rate (reversion risk)
- **Exit NOI**: Projected stabilized NOI at end of hold period
- **Selling Costs**: Brokerage commissions (1-2%), legal, closing costs

#### Best Practices

**Model Templates and Consistency**
- Create reusable templates for consistency across deals
- Standardize nomenclature, formatting, and calculations
- Facilitate model auditing and peer review
- Enable portfolio-level roll-ups

**Assumption Documentation**
- Clearly label and document all key assumptions
- Separate inputs from calculations
- Use assumption summary sheet
- Provide support for market-based assumptions (comps, broker quotes, reports)

**Cross-Checking and Validation**
- Reconcile model inputs to source documents (rent roll, operating statements)
- Validate totals and subtotals
- Check reasonableness of outputs against market benchmarks
- Third-party review for larger transactions

**Collaboration Across Teams**
- Gather input from development, leasing, asset management, property management
- Incorporate market intelligence from acquisitions and dispositions
- Legal review of lease terms and their financial implications
- Engineering input on CapEx requirements

### 3.3 Sensitivity Analysis and Scenario Planning

#### Stress Testing

**Purpose**: Evaluate how the investment performs under adverse conditions

**Common Stress Test Variables:**
- **Vacancy Rates**: Model 20-30% higher than base case
- **Rental Income**: 10-15% below projections
- **Expense Growth**: 10% higher than expected
- **CapEx Overruns**: 25-50% higher than budgeted
- **Exit Cap Rate**: 50-100 bps higher than assumed
- **Interest Rates**: 100-200 bps higher (for floating rate debt)
- **Lease-Up Timeline**: 6-12 months longer than projected

**Break-Even Analysis**
- Minimum occupancy for debt service coverage
- Maximum cap rate for target returns
- Required rental rate achievements

#### Scenario Modeling

**Best Case Scenario**
- Faster lease-up
- Higher rental rates
- Lower expenses
- Favorable exit timing and cap rate

**Base Case Scenario**
- Most likely outcomes based on market research
- Conservative but achievable assumptions
- Primary basis for investment decision

**Worst Case Scenario**
- Delayed lease-up
- Market rental rate decline
- Elevated expenses
- Extended hold period or challenged exit

**Strategic Alternative Scenarios**
- Refinancing at loan maturity vs. selling
- Different exit timing options
- Alternative value-add strategies
- Lease structure variations

#### Waterfall Analysis (for Joint Ventures)
- Preferred return structures
- Profit split tiers and promotes
- Capital account tracking
- Distribution priorities
- Clawback provisions

**Financial Analysis**
- Purchase price and capitalization

- Key assumptions and support
- Return metrics (IRR, equity multiple, COC, DSCR)
- Comparison to investment criteria
- Sensitivity analysis results

**Tenant/Lease Analysis**
- Rent roll summary
- Lease expiration schedule
- Tenant credit profiles
- Mark-to-market analysis
- Rollover risk assessment
- Leasing velocity assumptions

**Business Plan**
- Value-add strategies
- Repositioning plans

- Management approach
- Exit strategies

**Risk Analysis**
- Market risks
- Property-specific risks
- Tenant concentration and credit
- Execution risks
- Financing and interest rate risk
- Mitigation strategies for each

**Capital Structure**
- Equity requirement
- Debt terms and structure
- JV partnership structure (if applicable)
- Promote and waterfall

**Due Diligence Summary**
- Legal (title, survey, zoning)
- Environmental (Phase I/II findings)
- Physical (PCA highlights)
- Third-party report conclusions

**Transaction Timeline**
- Key milestones and deadlines
- Closing date
- Contingency expiration dates

#### IC Presentation Format

**Typical Deck Structure:**
1. Cover slide with property image and key metrics
2. Executive summary (1-2 slides)
3. Market overview (2-3 slides)
4. Property overview (2-3 slides)
5. Financial summary (2-3 slides)
6. Business plan (1-2 slides)
7. Risk analysis (1-2 slides)
8. Recommendation

**Best Practices:**
- Lead with the investment thesis
- Use visuals (maps, photos, charts) effectively
- Highlight key risks prominently
- Show sensitivity analysis
- Be prepared to defend assumptions
- Anticipate questions

#### IC Process

**Pre-Meeting**
- Distribute materials 3-5 days in advance
- Allow time for committee member review
- Field preliminary questions

**Meeting**
- Presentation by deal team (15-30 minutes)
- Q&A and discussion (30-60 minutes)
- Robust debate on merits encouraged
- "Devil's advocate" challenge of assumptions

**Decision**
- Approve: Proceed to final due diligence or closing
- Conditional Approval: Address specific items and re-present
- Reject: Pass on opportunity
- Table: Request additional information or analysis

**Post-Meeting**
- Document decision and rationale
- Communicate conditions or required follow-ups
- Update deal pipeline status

## 4. TRANSACTION EXECUTION

### 4.1 Purchase and Sale Agreement (PSA) Negotiation

#### Key Negotiated Terms

**Purchase Price and Earnest Money**
- Total consideration
- Earnest money deposit amount and timing (often 1-3% at PSA, additional at end of DD)
- Earnest money refundability conditions

**Due Diligence Period**
- Length (typically 30-60 days)
- "Free look" period where buyer can terminate for any reason
- Contingency items

## 5. PAIN POINTS AND BOTTLENECKS

### 5.1 Process Inefficiencies

#### Time-Consuming Manual Processes

**Document Review and Data Extraction**
- **Lease Abstraction**: Manually reading and extracting key terms from 50+ page lease documents for dozens or hundreds of tenants
- **Financial Statement Analysis**: Manual data entry from PDFs into Excel models
- **Rent Roll Reconciliation**: Cross-referencing rent rolls with leases and bank statements
- **Estoppel Collection**: Chasing down tenant responses, manual review and reconciliation
- **Third-Party Reports**: Reading lengthy engineering, environmental, and market reports to extract key findings

**Time Impact**: Lease abstraction alone can take 2-4 hours per lease; for a 50-tenant property, that's 100-200 hours of manual work.

#### Fragmented and Relationship-Dependent Processes

**Deal Sourcing**
- Fragmented, localized, relationship-driven process
- Requires outreach to multiple contacts across banks, lenders, brokers, insurance companies
- No centralized marketplace for many off-market opportunities
- Difficult to systematically track and score opportunities

**Information Silos**
- Data scattered across emails, file shares, deal rooms, PDFs
- No single source of truth for property information
- Difficult to search and retrieve historical information
- Knowledge loss when team members leave

#### Modeling Bottlenecks

**Excel-Dependent Workflows**
- Heavily reliant on proprietary Excel models
- Error-prone manual data entry
- Version control challenges
- Difficult to audit and peer review
- Limited collaboration capabilities
- No standardization across organization

**Assumption Documentation**
- Assumptions often buried in formulas or in analyst's head
- Lack of clear audit trail for decisions
- Difficult to update assumptions consistently across model
- Comparison across deals challenging

### 5.2 Due Diligence Pain Points

#### Document Management Challenges

**Volume and Variety**
- Hundreds to thousands of documents per transaction
- Multiple formats (PDF, Word, Excel, images, CAD drawings)
- Inconsistent naming and organization
- Large file sizes and storage requirements

**Data Room Navigation**
- Seller-provided data rooms often poorly organized
- Missing or mislabeled documents
- Version confusion (is this the current lease or an old one?)
- No standardization across transactions

**Document Chasing**
- Constant back-and-forth requesting missing items
- Seller delays in providing information
- Incomplete document sets
- Last-minute document dumps before DD deadline

#### Quality and Accuracy Issues

**Data Inconsistencies**
- Rent roll doesn't match lease terms
- Financial statements don't reconcile to rent roll
- Estoppels contradict lease terms or rent roll
- Engineering reports show different square footages than marketing materials

**Verification Burden**
- Every claim must be independently verified
- Trust but verify approach = double the work
- Bank statement review rarely possible
- Unaudited financial statements

#### Coordination Challenges

**Multi-Party Coordination**
- Synchronizing schedules across attorneys, engineers, inspectors, brokers, lenders
- Managing deadlines and deliverables from multiple vendors
- Chasing status updates
- Last-minute scheduling conflicts

**Internal Team Coordination**
- Legal, finance, asset management, acquisitions all need information
- Avoiding duplicate work
- Ensuring consistent communication with seller
- Tracking who has reviewed what

### 5.3 Underwriting and Analysis Bottlenecks

#### Assumption Development

**Market Research Time**
- Gathering comparable property data
- Researching submarket trends
- Validating rental rates and expenses
- Understanding tenant industries and credit

**Delayed Sensitivity Analysis**
- Often happens late in process due to time constraints
- Scenarios not fully explored before IC meeting
- Insufficient stress testing

#### Investment Committee Preparation

**Material Preparation Time**
- Significant time creating IC memo and presentation
- Much time spent on formatting and visual design
- Supporting exhibits and appendices
- Multiple rounds of internal review before IC

**Last-Minute Changes**
- New information emerges late in DD
- Model updates cascade through entire presentation
- Rush to update all materials
- Insufficient time to reconsider implications

### 5.4 Transaction Execution Delays

#### Negotiation Inefficiencies

**PSA Negotiation**
- Multiple rounds of redlines and comments
- Slow email back-and-forth
- Legal reviews create delays
- Misunderstandings requiring clarification

**Document Complexity**
- Loan documents voluminous and complex
- Multiple parties reviewing same documents
- Signature collection across entities
- Coordinating document execution

#### Closing Delays

**Common Causes:**
- Missing or incomplete tenant estoppels
- Title issues discovered late
- Lender delays in loan approval
- Seller's inability to deliver required documents
- Survey or engineering report issues
- Last-minute tenant issues or defaults

**Cascading Impacts:**
- Extension fees
- Financing rate lock expirations
- Market timing missed
- Other deal delays due to team capacity

### 5.5 Post-Closing Integration Challenges

**Information Transfer**
- Physical and digital file transfer from seller
- Missing or incomplete property records
- Institutional knowledge loss
- No standardized handover checklist

**System Setup**
- Property management software data entry
- Lease administration system loading
- Vendor management setup
- Accounting system configuration
